Maya Angelou

Maya Angelou was born Marguerite Johnson in St. Louis, Missouri, on April 4, 1928. She grew up in St. Louis, Missouri and Stamps, Arkansas. She was an author, poet, historian, songwriter, playwright, dancer, stage and screen producer, director, performer, singer, and civil rights activist. She won many, many awards and medals during her career including the Presidential Medal of Freedom presented by President Barack Obama in 2010. She was the first black woman director in Hollywood, and she wrote, produced, directed, and starred in productions for stage, film, and television. She also wrote and produced several prize-winning documentaries.

Angelou died on May 28, 2014, in Winston-Salem, North Carolina. She was eighty-six.
